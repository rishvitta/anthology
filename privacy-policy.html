02 Information We Collect

We collect personal information in connection with the services we provide and our business operations. The categories of information we may collect include:

From Clients and Prospective Clients

  • Business contact details (name, title, organisation, email, telephone number, address)
  • Information provided in Statements of Work, project briefs, and change orders
  • Billing and payment information sufficient to process invoices
  • Communications records, including emails and meeting notes
  • Confidential Information as defined under our Master Services Agreements

From Contractors and Candidates

  • Identity information (name, address, date of birth, government-issued identification as required)
  • Professional information (CV/résumé, work history, qualifications, references)
  • Tax identification and payment details (including information required for IRS Form 1099-NEC issuance)
  • Background check and drug test results, where required by a Client engagement
  • Right to work documentation

From Website Visitors

  • Information submitted through our contact form (name, organisation, email address, message)
  • Technical data such as IP address, browser type, and pages visited (collected via standard server logs)

03 How We Use Your Information

We use the personal information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To perform our contractual obligations under Master Services Agreements, Independent Contractor Agreements, and Right to Represent Agreements
  • To assess, place, and manage contractors and candidates for client engagements
  • To issue and process invoices, payments, and tax documentation
  • To conduct background screening and verification where required
  • To communicate with clients, contractors, and candidates regarding ongoing and prospective engagements
  • To protect Anthology's legitimate business interests, including the enforcement of non-solicitation and confidentiality obligations
  • To comply with applicable federal, state, and local laws and regulations
  • To respond to enquiries submitted via our website
  • To maintain the security and integrity of our systems and client data

04 Confidentiality and Information Security

Anthology treats all client, contractor, and candidate information as strictly confidential in accordance with the terms of our contractual agreements. Confidential Information is not disclosed to any third party without prior written consent, except:

  • Where disclosure is required by applicable law, regulation, or valid court order — in which case we will provide advance written notice to the affected party to the extent permitted by law
  • To Anthology's authorised personnel or subcontractors who require access in order to perform services, subject to binding confidentiality obligations
  • Where information has become generally publicly available through no breach by Anthology

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information against loss, theft, unauthorised access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification.

Trade Secrets

Anthology's confidentiality obligations with respect to information constituting a trade secret under applicable law survive the termination or expiration of any agreement until, if ever, such information loses its trade secret protection other than through an act or omission of Anthology.

Defend Trade Secrets Act Notice

Pursuant to the Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016, individuals will not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for any disclosure of a trade secret made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law, or in a complaint or other document filed under seal in a lawsuit or other proceeding.

05 Sharing and Disclosure of Information

Anthology does not sell personal information. We may disclose personal information in the following circumstances:

  • To Clients: Contractor and candidate information (including CVs, professional profiles, and Right to Represent documentation) is shared with Clients for the purpose of evaluating and engaging candidates for specific positions
  • To Subcontractors and Partners: Where Anthology engages third-party partners to assist in service delivery, such parties are subject to binding confidentiality obligations consistent with those in our client and contractor agreements
  • For Legal and Compliance Purposes: Where required by law, subpoena, court order, or regulatory requirement
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of our assets, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections

06 Intellectual Property and Work Product

Where Anthology creates Work Product or Deliverables for Clients, such materials may incorporate Client data and materials. Anthology treats all Client Materials as Confidential Information and uses them only to the extent necessary to perform the agreed Services.

All Work Product created by contractors engaged by Anthology is treated as work made for hire and vests in Anthology or the relevant Client as set out in the applicable agreement. Contractors acknowledge that Work Product containing or based on Client information is subject to the confidentiality obligations set out in their Independent Contractor Agreement.

07 Retention of Information

We retain personal information for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, to comply with our legal and contractual obligations, and to protect our legitimate business interests — including the enforcement of non-solicitation and intellectual property obligations which may survive contract termination.

Tax records and related financial documentation are retained for the minimum period required by applicable IRS and state tax regulations. Background check information is retained only for the period required by applicable law and the relevant Client engagement.
